From Trial Balance to Investment-Grade Databook in 7 Steps

Published 2025 • 6 min read

Investors rarely read 100-page packs. What they want is a databook that reconciles cleanly, highlights KPIs, and can be trusted to drive valuation. Here’s our field-tested 7-step playbook.

1) Map the Trial Balance to Reporting Lines

Map TB accounts into standardized P&L and BS headings. Align with IFRS/IND-AS, strip non-recurring items, and keep a COA mapping table for repeatability.

2) Flux Checks (MoM & YoY)

Run % and absolute variance checks on revenue, COGS, OPEX, and balance captions to catch anomalies before investors do.

3) Working Capital Logic

Build AR/AP/Inventory roll-forwards that reconcile to closing balances. Derive DSO, DPO, DIO, and cash conversion cycle.

4) Net Debt Proof

Summarize facilities, reconcile to ledgers, adjust for leases, and present cash vs OD with a clear net-debt bridge.

5) Cohort & Margin Diagnostics

For SaaS/consumer, retention and margin evolution matter. Show LTV:CAC, churn, payback, and GM progression by cohort.

6) Sensitivities & Scenarios

Stress-test with 2–3 scenarios (e.g., churn +10%, delayed collections) and show impact on cash and covenants.

7) Executive Note

Close with a 2–3 page note summarizing red flags, adjustments, and upside levers—the section partners actually read.
